I built {{ BANK_NAME }} to showcase how far you can take a credible banking UI in the open — readable typography, dark-first layout, and real flows (not just mock screens). Explore the live app, read the code, and treat it as a learning sandbox: it is not a licensed institution and holds no real money.

Why this project exists

Most portfolio pieces stop at a single screen. {{ BANK_NAME }} goes further: authentication, a ledger-style transfer path, exports, and clear customer flows — the kinds of seams you hit in real product work, packaged as a cohesive demo you can run locally.

Use it to study patterns, borrow ideas for your own apps, or show recruiters a live link that actually does something when you click around. Contributions and feedback are welcome; the goal is curiosity, not compliance-grade banking.

What you can explore

Sign in or open an account — core paths are implemented end-to-end in this demo. Each area maps to real views and the database (demo data only).

Virtual cards

Create demo virtual debit cards, set daily and monthly spend limits, freeze or unfreeze anytime, and run a simulated card payment that debits your main balance.

Biometric login & payments

Use WebAuthn passkeys — Face ID, Touch ID, or Windows Hello — for passwordless sign-in. Optionally confirm transfers with your passkey instead of a one-time code (enable in Settings).

Transfers & IBAN

Send money by account number or {{ BANK_NAME }} IBAN, preview activity after you pay, save frequent recipients as beneficiaries, and get a receipt for sent payments.

Statements & export

Browse your activity by timeline and balance-after rows — handy for demos and portfolio walkthroughs; CSV export labels are surfaced in the UI.

OTP verification

Sign up with email and phone, confirm with a 6-digit code (demo-friendly; never ship OTP hints in production APIs).

Install as an app (PWA)

Add {{ BANK_NAME }} to your home screen or desktop from the browser — standalone icon, theme toggle, feels closer to a native banking app.

Secure sessions

Django sessions, hashed passwords, and login-required routes — customer areas stay behind authentication in this demo.

Admin console (demo)

Use Django admin to inspect models and balances — for portfolio demos only, not real operations.
